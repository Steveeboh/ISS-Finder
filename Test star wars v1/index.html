The International Space Station is not just a bright dot moving across the night sky; it's a monumental achievement of international collaboration and engineering. Orbiting approximately 250 miles (400 kilometers) above Earth at a speed of about 17,500 miles per hour (28,000 kilometers per hour), the ISS completes an orbit around our planet every 90 minutes. Since its inception in 1998, the station has been continuously inhabited, serving as a microgravity and space environment research laboratory where astronauts conduct experiments in biology, physics, astronomy, and more. It's a testament to what humanity can accomplish when we work together across borders.
